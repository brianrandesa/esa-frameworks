The ESA Close

Where the deal is won or lost · 6 moves · ask, then go silent
← all frameworks
The truth this framework fixes: You build trust faster than anyone — and then leave the money on the table at the ask. The close isn't more rapport. It's the courage to ask and shut up. And remember: if your service changes their business, not closing them is the unkind act. The ask is the gift.

Front-load the trust you're great at. Force the ask you skip. Six moves.

1Reconnect30 sec
Warm and brief. You've already built the relationship — don't rebuild it.
Say: "Great to see you again — I've been thinking about this since we talked, genuinely fired up about what we can build."
2Recap & Surface1 min
One-breath recap, then flush hidden objections to the top before they ambush the close.
Say: "Quick recap — we build your webinar machine end to end and fill it with the right people. Before anything else: since we talked, did anything come up, any hesitation? Or are you ready to get started?"
3The Number, Clean90 sec
Run the math ONCE, slow, on screen. Then the ROI flip — and go quiet. The single most important 90 seconds; don't muddy it.
ROI flip: "What's one client worth to you over a year?" then silent … "Right — so this pays for itself on the very first one."
4Temp Checkthe gate
The gate. 8+ → go straight to the ask. Under 8 → find the one thing and handle it.
Ask: "On a 1–10, how ready are you to finally get the right people in the room?" … If under 8: "What's the gap between that and a 10?"
5THE ASK — the move you keep skippingthe moment
Say it directly. Then stop talking. Whoever speaks first after the ask loses — let it be them.
Say: "You're clearly ready, and I'd be doing you a disservice to send you off to figure this out alone for another year. Let's lock it in today — $9,800 to get started, we launch in 45 days. Run the Affirm now so you keep your cash for ads, or pay the invoice in full — which do you want to do?" 🤐 NOW STOP TALKING
6Lock It — close it, or BOOK the closenever "later"
Get the commitment ON the call. If you genuinely can't — never leave with "process it when you get home." That's the callback that fades. A deferred close is a booked close, never a hope. Walk down this ladder in order:
1 · Close it now: "Perfect — sending the link right now, let's knock it out while we're on so you're officially in. Then I'll introduce you to Michaela today and she starts your onboarding."
2 · Can't pay live? (no card, bad signal, driving, needs a partner) — BOOK THE CLOSE, don't "follow up": "Let's not do this half-blind — tomorrow at [time], you're at your computer with your card, I'll have everything pulled up, and we get you started. I'm sending the agreement + link now so it's ready to go. Locked in?" get a TIME, not a "later"
3 · Last resort — micro-commitment: "Before you go, let's at least start the Affirm so we hold your 45-day window. 90 seconds now." A booked close ≈ 70% follow-through. "I'll process it later" ≈ 20%.
⚠ Protect the environment: never run a $9.8K close with someone who can't hear you or doesn't have their card on them. If the setting's broken, that IS the move — "let's get you to a computer and do this right," then book the exact time. A bad close environment is a reason to schedule, not to wing it.

Objection cheat-card — the four you'll hear most at $9.8K

"I don't have the cash""That's exactly what Affirm's for — let's run it right now, 90 seconds, and you keep your cash for the ad spend."
"Let me think about it""Totally fair — what specifically? If it's whether it works, that's what the 45-day build and the math are for. If it's trust, you already feel we're the right people. So what's the real hesitation?"
"I need to talk to my partner""Of course — take 60 seconds, I'll hop off camera. One question first: if you both say yes, any reason we wouldn't start today?"
"Can you do it cheaper?""I hold the price because dropping it means dropping the standard — and your results are what my reputation rides on. What we can do is Affirm so the cash is easy."

The 5 cardinal rules

  • ❌ Don't teach. You already taught — more teaching means less buying.
  • ❌ Don't drop the price. Ever. No downsell.
  • ✅ Ask, then go silent. The silence does the closing.
  • ✅ The ask is service, not taking. You'd be doing them a disservice not to.
  • ✅ Claims stay FTC-safe — illustrative industry ranges, never "you'll make $X."
  • ✅ A deferred close is a BOOKED close — never "process it later." Set the time, card ready, link already sent. If the setting's broken, that's a reason to schedule, not to wing it.
