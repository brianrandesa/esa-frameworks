The $15K Webinar Offer — Sales Call Framework

9-step flow · pain & urgency married to proof & posture · the same clean call, every closer · $15,000 build + 10% of back-end
The 7 rules that hold on every call:  1) Lead with truth, never guarantee.  2) Check real engagement (story views, likes, comments) — not follower count.  3) Same numbers every time: 165-point checklist · $30–50 / registrant · 30% show · 30% book · 30% close.  4) Posture — "I don't need it that bad that I'd take it without knowing we can help you."  5) FTC-safe language, always — illustrative ranges, never "you'll make $X."  6) Never drop the price — no downsell.  7) Ask for the sale, then go silent — and never hang up without booking the next step.
Why this order: the first half digs the pain and the cost of staying stuck so the prospect feels the gap. Then the math and the offer land with fuel behind them — instead of cold logic they can walk away from.
1Frame & Earn Permission1 min
Set posture and control upfront. You're qualifying them as much as they're qualifying you.
Say: "Before we go down the rabbit hole, I want to ask you some questions to make sure this is even a good fit. I don't like taking people's money unless I know we can actually help them. If it's a fit, I'll tell you exactly how we'd do it. If it's not, I'll tell you that too. Cool?"
2Current Situation — Discovery6–8 min
Get the facts. Where are they now, and how warm is the audience? This decides the whole call.
  • Have you done an event before? Live or virtual? Free or paid? How did people find out?
  • What's your audience — email list, social? Have them screen-share live and check real engagement (likes, comments, story views), not follower count.
  • What's the webinar idea / the hook? What's the back-end offer you'd sell?
  • Do you have 5–10 case studies / proof you can show?
  • Where do your clients come from right now? (Listen for "referrals.")
3Problem / Pain / Challenge3–4 min
Excavate the REAL pain — ask, don't tell. For most prospects it's the same: no predictable way to get clients. Make them say it out loud.
Ask: "When's the last time you got a client that wasn't a referral?" … "So everything rides on who happens to send someone your way."
Ask: "What happens to the business when referrals slow down? What's that uncertainty cost you — in revenue, in stress, in being able to plan?"
Reflect it back: "So the real problem isn't your topic or your skill — it's that you have no lever you can pull to grow on demand. You're at the mercy of referrals. Is that fair to say?"
4Solution — The Reframe2 min
Show what solving it looks like. Shift them off "a stage" and onto "a predictable list."
Say: "A webinar isn't a stage — it's a lead-gen machine, and it's the lever you've been missing. You're not getting 10 booked calls. You're getting 100 business owners raising their hand. The 70 who don't show and the 20 who don't book are all leads — we call and text every one when they register, before, and after. That's a tap you can turn on whenever you want."
The ladder (protects your reputation): "We're virtual-first now. We don't even touch an in-person event until you've proven $100K on a virtual one — because if we can't fill a webinar, a live event is just a way to lose money. So we perfect it virtually first, then climb to live. The webinar is rung one."
5Future Pace / Goal + ROI Flip2–3 min
Paint the future state, then make them quantify the gap. Let them say the number.
Paint it: "Picture being able to fill a room with 100 of your ideal buyers whenever you choose. You stop praying for referrals and start deciding when you grow. What would that change for you?"

The ROI Flip — ask this, then go quiet

"What's your average client worth to you over a year?"
Then: "So if I put 100 of those buyers in a room and you close even 2 of them — what did this just do for you? The $15,000 is a rounding error against a single client."

6 · Consequence — the cost of staying stuck

This is the urgency engine. Don't skip it. Make the cost of doing nothing bigger than the price.

Say: "If nothing changes, where's the business a year from now? Same spot — same dependency, same hoping the phone rings. And you'll likely have spent more than $15,000 on marketing experiments that went nowhere anyway. The cost here isn't the fee. It's another year stuck at the mercy of referrals."
7Transition30 sec
A deliberate bridge from discovery into the pitch.
Say: "Based on everything you just told me, here's exactly what we'd build for you and how it works."

Pitch the 3–4 pillars (the what), then the deliverables (the how), then prove it with the math. "Build the house, hand you the keys."
1 · Fill the room — ads + messaging + the lead-gen machine 2 · The system — funnel, CRM, automations, speed-to-lead 3 · Set you up to convert — deck, framework, 100-lead follow-up 4 · Hand you the keys + the data — account manager, weekly calls, the numbers
  • We build it A→Z, from concept to live — full virtual-event funnel + CRM (registration, reminders, replay, all follow-up)
  • Front-end ad creative + setup + we run the ads on their account
  • Messaging + positioning — the hook that gets cold buyers to show up
  • Full slide deck + the webinar framework, built for you
  • All SMS + email + an AI bot that works the leads (register, pre-event, post-event)
  • Day-of: one of us on the call with you, all tech tested and run, while you present
  • Weekly 1-on-1 build calls + dedicated account manager (Michaela) + daily Slack with the whole team
  • 165-point ClickUp checklist (you get view access) + an 8-phase QA process — you approve each phase before we move on

Skin in the game: "We take 10% of the back-end above the $15K — so if you don't make your investment back, we don't take a percentage. We only win when you win."
What we DON'T do: present the webinar (that's you — your expertise) or make the back-end sales calls (also you). We get the right people in the room and set you up to convert.

9 · The Deal — Temp Check → Handle → ASK → Lock

1 · Temp check (the gate): "On a scale of 1–10, how ready are you to finally get the right people in the room?" 8+ → go to the ask. Under 8 → "What's the gap between that and a 10?" and handle that.

2 · The real objection: a price stall is usually not price — it's "I don't know if the market wants what I sell."

Name it: "You know better than I do — does the marketplace want what you have, and are they already paying you for it? If yes, $15,000 is a drop in the bucket. If there's hesitation, let's figure out where it's coming from before you spend a dollar."

3 · THE ASK — say it, then go silent (whoever talks first loses):

Say: "You're clearly ready, and I'd be doing you a disservice to send you off to figure this out alone for another year. Let's lock it in today — $7,500 to start, we launch in 45 days. We can run the Affirm right now so you keep your cash for ad spend, or do the invoice — which do you want to do?"  🤐 Then STOP talking.

4 · Handle the two you'll hear most:

"I don't have the cash": "That's exactly what Affirm's for — let's run it right now, 90 seconds, and you keep your cash for the ads."    "Let me think about it": "Totally fair — what specifically? If it's whether it works, that's what the 45-day build and the math are for. If it's trust, you already feel we're the right people. So what's the real hesitation?"

5 · No downsell. $15K or they walk — never a $2,500 safety net that cannibalizes the offer. Hold the line.

6 · LOCK THE NEXT STEP — every time: get the Affirm app started or the $7,500 invoice paid on the call. If they truly can't: "Before you go — let's at least start the Affirm so we hold your 45-day window. 90 seconds now." A foot in the door beats a cold callback. Booked/started ≈ 70% follow-through vs. ≈ 20% on "let's reconnect."
