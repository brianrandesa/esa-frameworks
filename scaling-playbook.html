The 8-Figure Sales Team Blueprint

Notes from Jacob Mercier's free course (a fractional sales agency that's done $40M+ for clients) — and a straight read on what it adds to ESA's pre / during / post / reporting systems.
← all frameworks

TL;DR — is it worth our time?

Yes. His call framework is ~90% identical to ours (same bones: Frame → Situation → Problem → Solution → Future Pace → Consequence → Transition → Pitch → Close). That's validation. The real value isn't the call — it's the team infrastructure around it: a deeper reporting system, a cleaner pipeline, and the recruiting / onboarding / comp / management machine for scaling past a solo closer.

  • Call: a few sharp upgrades — an explicit "create-doubt" solution step, a pillar-based pitch, and a tie-down-before-price sequence.
  • Reporting: his biggest add. Efficiency + capacity metrics we don't track yet (cash per call booked, cash-to-rev ratio, slot utilization) + a forecasting model.
  • Team: the entire engine for getting you out of the closer seat — which is the whole point.
The line that matters most for you: his #1 reason businesses stall at the $100K–500K/mo ceiling is "you're still the chief sales officer." Revenue capped by your personal time and bandwidth. That's the exact stall — the move past it is the system below, not more of your hours on the phone.

His blueprint in one screen

1 · Offer (3S + TAM)

  • Specific person · specific problem · specific solution. Market big enough for paid ads ($50–100M/yr spend, 30–50K+ buyers). Health / wealth / relationships. Ticket $3–20K.

2 · Marketing — 3 proven funnels

  • VSL → application → book a call (best for cold + a setter team).
  • Low-ticket ascension ($30–100 front end + bumps to liquidate ad spend, then book buyers).
  • Live webinar / challenge → book-a-call or mid-ticket → high-ticket. High conversion, needs a great live presenter (the owner). (This is our lane.)

3 · Recruiting

  • Ideal Sales Rep Profile (the ICP mirror — who connects with our buyer). Study your top performers' commonalities. Long-form application + Loom video + skill assessment (he prefers call reviews over roleplay) + interview → offer.
  • Rule of two — always hire in pairs. Always be recruiting — keep a bench even when not hiring.

4 · Onboarding & ramp (turn a hire into KPI in 3–4 weeks)

  • The Sales Playbook (offer knowledge, scripts, CRM training, KPIs, SOPs, call library, culture) — build once, evergreen.
  • Stages: Day 1–3 orientation → Day 4–7 script mastery + shadow → Wk 2 half-volume live calls → Day 21–30 KPI validation. Speed of feedback is everything; get them on calls fast. "A maybe rep is a no 90% of the time."

5 · Compensation

  • Closers ~10% cash collected (sliding scale 8→15%). Setters base + 2–5%. Bonus contingencies (e.g. cash-to-rev ratio gate) so reps can't game the comp plan.

6 · Systems — meetings, CRM, data, forecasting

  • Daily meetings (wins → announcements → projections → training/call reviews). CRM smart-lists for setters, staged pipeline for closers. Manual-ish tracking (Sheets/Airtable) + EOD reports → Slack. Forecast = capacity × cash per call booked.

7 · Sales process & philosophy

  • Taylor Welsh's 3 rules: help them make the best decision for them; the best is outside their comfort zone; they'll fight to stay in it. Internal pressure (their own pain/urgency) over external pressure (sleazy tactics). Sell the A→B gap; people buy the outcome, not the product.

8 · Sales management

  • Owns team performance, accountability, coaching, pipeline, forecasting, marketing feedback, culture. A great manager 10×'s a team. Daily/weekly/monthly responsibilities. Coaches on data, not emotion.

9 · Culture & leadership

  • "You get the culture you tolerate." Recognition + friendly competition + meritocracy. Leadership is the ultimate retention — money keeps reps happy, leadership keeps them loyal when months get lean.

What it ADDS — Pre-call new

  • Setter smart-lists / lead priority order — when we add setters, work leads warmest→coldest: speed-to-lead (new opt-ins) → no-show <2wk → cancelled <21d → unclosed 21–90d → cold 90d+. Directly relevant to clearing the dirty pipeline.
  • Otherwise it confirms our pre-call brief. No change needed to the solo-closer prep.

What it ADDS — During-call new

  • An explicit "create-doubt" solution step (between Pain and Future Pace): ask what they've already tried, then make them admit it won't get them there. This pre-handles objections before the pitch. We fold this into the Reframe — worth making it its own beat.
  • Pitch as 3–4 pillars, each tied to a pain they named, with a time-check between each ("does that make sense? any questions?"). Keeps them tracking instead of monologuing. Sharper than our "pitch + math."
  • Tie-down / temp-check BEFORE the price drop — clear every doubt so the only thing left to handle is money + logistics. Then drop price calm, matter-of-fact, "stupid simple." Then assumptive close ("ready to get started?").
  • Philosophy layer (Taylor Welsh 3 rules + internal-vs-external pressure) — this reinforces our FTC-safe posture: build internal urgency through their own pain, never sleazy external "price goes up tomorrow" tactics.

What it ADDS — Post-call new

  • A more granular closer pipeline than our 3 paths: Booked → No-show → Cancelled → Follow-up booked → Hot (<2wk) → Warm (2–4wk) → Long-term (30d+) → Deposit → Sale → DQ/bad-fit. This is the cure for the 643-opp mess and a more precise version of our Path B.
  • EOD report per rep (Google/Type form → Slack channel): qualitative (mood, objections, what happened) + quantitative (the day's numbers). We don't have this — it's the daily heartbeat that catches drift early.
  • CRM hygiene rules: no deal without a note + a next action + the right stage + the source. Make it enforceable.

What it ADDS — Reporting system biggest gap

Our scoreboard tracks 10 surface counts. His system tracks efficiency and capacity — the metrics that actually tell you where the money leaks and let you forecast.

MetricWhy it matters for ESA
Cash collected per call booked ★The north star. Divide your cost-per-booked-call into it = ROAS per appointment. Tells you who the best closer really is (not just close rate).
Cash-to-revenue ratioCollections health. Low = over-discounting, too many payment plans, or weak objection handling. (Right at the heart of our A/R problem.)
Slot utilization %Capacity planning — booked calls ÷ available slots. Tells you: hire, buy more leads, or trim.
Schedule-to-closeEncompasses show rate × close rate in one number.
Offer rate% of live calls you actually pitched. Below ~80% = you're DQing/protecting time too much.
The forecasting model (use this for the $1M/mo plan): capacity (reps × calls/day × days) × cash collected per call booked = forecast. Then find the one bottleneck (leads, reps, show rate, close rate, or cash/call) and reverse-engineer to the target. This is the exact math to size the scale plan.

What we ALREADY have keep

  • The 9-step call framework (his and ours line up) + the close, temp-check, and "ask then silence."
  • The proposal / contract / invoice paperwork + the FTC-safe language.
  • The post-call money sequence + the Yes / Follow-Up / DQ branching + the GHL automations.
  • The KPI scoreboard (the foundation his deeper metrics plug into).

Verdict — what to steal now

  1. Upgrade the call (cheap, immediate): add the create-doubt step, the pillar pitch with time-checks, and the tie-down→price→assumptive-close sequence to the during-call checklist.
  2. Deepen the pipeline (fixes the dirty-pipeline problem): adopt his closer stages — Hot / Warm / Long-term split inside our Follow-Up path.
  3. Upgrade reporting (highest leverage): add cash-per-call-booked, cash-to-rev ratio, and slot utilization to the scoreboard, and stand up EOD reports → Slack.
  4. Build the team engine (the actual ceiling-breaker): the Sales Playbook + Ideal Sales Rep Profile + ramp stages + a manager — so the business stops being capped by your hours. This is the whole reason the system exists.
