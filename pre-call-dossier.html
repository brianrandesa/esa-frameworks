instant read 3 · The 30-Second Verdict

🟢 DREAM LEAD — green event type + backend ($5–25K or $25K+) + goal $100K+ + seats 200+ + 1–3 months. Fast close, big upside. Bring your A-game and assume the close.
🟡 QUALIFY HARD — any of: "Building one now" / "No backend," goal "Under $25K," "Under 50 seats," "Within 30 days" (rush), or "No date set." There's a yellow flag — dig on the call before you pitch $9.8K.
🔴 LIKELY DQ — Wedding / Social / DJ / Nightlife event types, OR "No backend" + goal "Under $25K." No model fit. Be gracious, refer out, don't burn the slot.

pre-calc 5 · ROI — Ready Before You Dial

Formula: seats × conservative backend close-rate × their backend value = revenue range vs. their $[goal].
Worked example (50–200 seats, $5–25K backend): fill ~100 attendees → even a 5% backend take at $9.8K = ~$75K — well past a $25–100K goal, against a $9.8K build.
Always frame as illustrative industry ranges — never "you'll make $X." Have it ready so you're not doing math live.

verdict 6 · Fit, Angle & Confirm-Live

Overall fit: 🟢 / 🟡 / 🔴  ·  The one hook for this prospect: [____]

Confirm live — the form doesn't capture these, and they decide the deal

☐ Audience / list size — can they actually fill the room?

☐ Ad budget — we need $5K minimum per event. Can they fund it?

☐ Live or virtual? — routes the offer ($9.8K webinar build vs. live ticket-fill).

☐ Is the backend real & ready? (especially "building one now")

☐ Is the date locked?

Form gap to close: the two biggest predictors of success — audience size and ad budget — aren't on the form. Either add them ("Roughly how big is your audience / list?" and "What's your monthly ad budget?") or keep them as Viktor-pulled intel + a live confirm. My rec: add audience size to the form (it filters tire-kickers), keep ad budget for the call (it's a money conversation you want to have live).
